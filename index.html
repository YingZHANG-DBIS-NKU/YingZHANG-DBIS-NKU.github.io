I am Ying ZHANG, a Professor in the College of Computer Science at Nankai University. My research focuses on Knowledge-Centric Intelligent Computing, investigating how structured knowledge and multimodal information can enhance the reasoning capability and controllability of large-scale intelligent systems.

Research Interests

Large Language Model Adaptation:​ I focus on post-training and adaptation methods for foundation models, including reinforcement-based fine-tuning and continual learning mechanisms, thereby enhancing model controllability, stability, and long-term adaptability in dynamic real world.

Knowledge Graphs:​ I study methods for constructing and evolving large-scale knowledge graphs, including knowledge extraction, knowledge alignment, and leveraging structured knowledge to enhance downstream tasks, aiming to improve interpretability and structured intelligence in complex environments.

Multimodal Information Processing:​ I explore joint modeling and cross-modal understanding across text, vision, audio, and structured data, with the goal of enabling robust semantic alignment and unified representation in open-domain scenarios.

Prospective Students

I am looking for highly self-driven Master’s and Ph.D. students, as well as undergraduate research interns. Students with strong backgrounds or interests in large language model alignment, knowledge graphs, and multimodal learning are encouraged to contact me with their CV and a brief research statement.
